Three key differences between absolute, relative, and fixed positioning

What happens to the content after the element?

Absolute: Content after the element will ignore the positioned element

Relative: Content after the element will leave space for the positioned element

Fixed: Content after the element will ignore the positioned element

Do they have a width, or do they shrink-wrap?

Absolute: Initially shrink-wrapped but can be sized or stretched

Relative:

  • If they are block level, they will initially be stretched but they can be given size.
  • If they are inline, they will initially be shrink-wrapped and cannot be given size.

Fixed: Initially shrink-wrapped but can be sized or stretched.

What are they positioned against?

Absolute: Positioned relative to a parent with positioning or viewport if no positioned parent is present.

Relative: Positioned relative to element's original position.

Fixed: Positioned relative to the viewport.
